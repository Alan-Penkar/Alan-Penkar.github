About Me

I received my B.S. in Applied Mathematics from Georgia Tech in 2010. After that I spent seven years consulting in workforce analytics and cloud integrations primarily with Deloitte Consulting. After this successful experience I went back to my passion for computers and mathematics, to pursue an M.S. in Analytics full-time. My focus areas are machine learning methods and bayesian statistics, and I will highlight some of that work on the blog in the months to come.

While pursuing my degree I am also a member of the Georgia Tech Robocup Team. This is an ongoing opportunity to tackle larger programming problems that require integrating several languages in a large codebase. Working with a number of very intelligent students in software, mechanical, and electrical engineering helps to broaden my understanding of machines and improve my skills as a maker while working on problems in computer vision, AI, robotics, and multi-agent collaboration.

Some of my personal interests include computers and rPi/Arduino powered devices. I love tinkering with electronics in my spare time. I've been skiing since I was four, and it remains my favorite winter activity. My favorite mountain is Whistler/Blackcomb, but having moved back to the southeast it may be some time before I'm able to get back there. I also play inline and ice hockey on the weekends and am an avid Dallas Stars fan. You can often catch my dog, Leopold the Great (Pyrenees), and I on the couch watching hockey, while I rub his belly.
